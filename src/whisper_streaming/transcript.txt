 We hold these truths to be self-evident that all men are treated equal, that they are endowed by their people with certain unalienable rights, that among these are life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness, that to secure these powers from the consent of the governed, that whenever any form of government becomes destructive of these ends. of the people to alter or abolish it, and to institute new government. learning its foundation on such principles and organizing its powers in such form. shall seem most likely to affect their safety and happiness.
